# Framing Security Threat

<h1>Framing Security Threats in a Bipolar World Order: A Transformer-Based Multimodal Analysis of Chinese and US News Media</h1>

**Abstract**<br />Addressing security threat framing in news coverage in the wake of global media diplomacy based on the assumption of a bipolar world order, this paper contributes to the field of International Relations supported by a computational media analysis based on textual and visual data using RoBERTa and CLIP. Viewing the Global South as a world region in constant struggle for power and economic agency detached from global powers, the present study investigates security threat framing in Chinese and US news coverage. Findings indicate that media possesses an important role in laying a ground for securitization on the basis of security threat framing. Further, findings indicate rivalry between China and the US rather than between the Global South and the Global North.
